Features

Everything a bike race promoter needs.
Nothing they don't.

Built for the person who drives the van, sets up the course, handles registration, AND runs the timing table. All at once.

Live RFID timing

AION Chip Sync connects your hardware to the cloud. Sub-second lap detection, multi-antenna support, real-time leaderboard for every category simultaneously.

Live GPS tracking

Racers opt in via QR code at the start line — no app install needed. Staff monitor all positions live. Spectators watch from anywhere.

Day-of registration

Walk-up racers added in seconds on any device. Self-serve registration via race code — racers sign up themselves, no staff needed.

Live momentum analytics

Track whether each racer's laps are getting faster or slower in real time. Animated trend arrows show pace direction on the live leaderboard.

Series & auto points

Championship standings update automatically after every race. Custom points per race type per category. Go home earlier — no spreadsheets required.

Works on any device

Browser-based. No app installs, no special laptops. Time from a tablet, share results via QR, parents check standings on their phones.

Social sharing cards

Post-race result cards designed for Instagram and Facebook. Every racer gets shareable content — builds your community with zero extra effort.

Why AION

Time isn't just a number. It's a story.

AION comes from the ancient Greek αἰών — not clock time (chronos), but the continuous, unbroken flow of time. The kind that connects every lap you've ever ridden into a single arc.

Chronos vs AION

Chronos is the clock — minutes and seconds. AION is the journey — every race, every rival, every improvement across a season. We track both, but we care about the second one.

AI that reads the race

Our AI doesn't just report results — it reads the race. Who pushed whom. Who faded. Who found a gear in the final lap. Context that turns a timestamp into a story worth sharing.

Every racer, every race

AION remembers. A junior racer's first podium. A masters rider's season-best lap. The moment someone finally beat their rival. The full arc — not just today's snapshot.

"We named it AION because great races deserve more than a spreadsheet. They deserve a story."

About us

Built by riders.
For riders.

We're avid cyclists, racers, parents, and coaches who've spent enough weekends on cold start lines and late nights entering race results into spreadsheets to know there had to be a better way.

AION started with a simple belief — more kids and adults should be out on bikes, having fun at local races. But race promotion is hard. The logistics are brutal. The admin is thankless. And the engagement usually dies the moment everyone loads up their cars and drives home.

We built AION to change that. Not just to make timing easier — but to use race data to spark real engagement before, during, and after every event. The banter from lap splits. The rivalry that builds over a season. The moment a kid sees their name in an AI-generated race story and suddenly can't wait for the next race.
