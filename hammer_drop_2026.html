How Points Eligibility Works
01
You choose your level at registration
When you register on BikeReg, you select your skill level — beginner, intermediate, advanced, or pro. That selection determines which race (20 min or 30 min) you are points-eligible in each night.
02
One points race per night, no exceptions
You can only earn series points in one race per event night. If you've already scored points in your eligible race, riding the other race counts as training miles only — you appear in results but are not scored.
03
Cross-racing for training is welcome
Want extra miles? Enter both races on a given night. Just know only your category-eligible race counts for points. The other race is listed as Not Points Eligible (NPE) in results.
04
Race director may adjust your category
At their discretion, the race director may ask a rider to move from the 20 min points race to the 30 min points race. This keeps competition appropriate and fair for everyone on the course.
RD decisions are final and made in the interest of fair competition for all riders.

How progression is measured
A proprietary performance improvement score calculated across the full series — not a single race result
01
Tracks your trajectory
Results are charted race over race to measure how your performance trend lines move throughout the full season.
02
Relative to the field
Improvement is measured against the rest of the field — not just your own times — so race conditions and competition strength are factored in.
03
Every race counts
The algorithm looks at your complete body of work across all Hammer Drop events — no single standout result wins this award.
The spirit of this award
The Wahoo Fitness Progression Award exists to celebrate growth over glory. You don't need to win a race. You don't need to podium. You just need to show up, race hard, and get better — and the data will tell the story. This award honors the rider who commits to the process and earns it lap by lap.
